# Why V300 Is Not Final

V300 stays as a checkpoint, not mentor-ready final evidence.

The decisive problem is not whether the pack can be opened. The current pack is readable, but the evidence stack is internally inconsistent:

- the repository-side `reports/V300000000000000000_final_status.json` says mentor-ready,
- the archive-side `reports/V300000000000000000_final_status.json` says `TRUE_EXTERNAL_HARD_BLOCK_REQUIRES_USER_ACTION`,
- the requirement audit says all checks passed,
- the completion audit says the final state is not stable.

That is enough to fail closed.

The second problem is visual. The main board is still not a clean mentor scene:

- the human reads as a sideways or low-confidence point-cloud body,
- the environment is present but too weak to feel like a real scene context,
- true / baseline / posthoc / same-topology / tiny / shuffled are too close in the image,
- the board is not yet the kind of full-scene RGB point cloud a mentor can judge at a glance.

The third problem is local evidence. The V160 close-ups are projection-assisted crops, not true local 3D crops:

- head/hair can support contour and hair-region claims only,
- hand/arm does not prove a hand shape,
- clothing does not prove a clear boundary improvement,
- facial detail is not visible enough to claim.

So the route must move on to true 3D morphology detail, with projection kept as auxiliary evidence only.
